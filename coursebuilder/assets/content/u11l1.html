ASSESSMENT-BASED GRADING

Many assessment experts believe that that most appropriate way to helping teachers impact achievement is helping them (a) improve the quality of their classroom assessments, (b) use those assessments to assign grades, and (c) ensure that there is a correlation between grades and external achievement. Simply put, students who do relatively well on classroom assessments should do relatively well on external achievement tests. This assignment will help you learn to do that. You will walk through the steps of conducting assessment-based grading for your lesson or unit.

1. Read/Review Chapter 16. Read the introduction and Purpose of Grading Carefully

2. LESSON AND CONTEXT SUMMARY. Summarize the lesson you will use and your instructional context and role as it relates to assessment-based grading.

3. GOAL-ATTAINMENT GRADING SCENARIO. Describe how you would carry out goal-attainment grading for your lesson by describing and/or doing each of the following:

A. CLARIFY YOUR CURRICULAR AIMS. Clarify your curricular aims, state which aspects of doing so are most important, and do so for your lesson.

B. CHOOSE GOAL-ATTAINMENT EVIDENCE. Clarify your goal-attainment evidence, clearing showing which the important aspects of this evidence.

C. WEIGHT GOAL-ATTAINMENT EVIDENCE. Weight your goal-attainment evidence, clearly showing the important aspects of doing so.

D. ARRIVE AT GRADE. Describe how you would arrive at a final goal-attainment grade.

4. GRADE-GIVING SPECIFICS. Read about (a) different approaches to grading. (b) evaluative options, (c) effort, (d) intangibles, (e) record keeping, (f) grading conferences, and (g) imprecision. Work the three most relevant ideas into your scenario above

5. EVALUATIVE OPTIONS. Read the section on Evaluative Options. Work the relative big ideas into the scenario above. Then write brief summaries of each of the three and rank order them in terms of relevance to you.

6. BIG IDEAS ABOUT ASSESSMENT-BASED GRADING. Review What to Classroom Teachers Need to Know and the Summary. State the three most relevant big ideas about assessment-based grading for you and people in your role. Make it clear why the first one is most relevant.
