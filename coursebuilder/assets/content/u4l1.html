Performance & Portfolio Assessment (Ch 8 & 9, June 2)

In this assignment, you will make either a performance assessment or a portfolio assessment and study the guidelines for both.

  • This is a more complicated assignment than the previous one. You will need to return to some of the earlier parts because they will not fully make sense until after your work on the later parts.
  • Your are responsible for the guidelines and advantages and disadvantages of both formats though you will only create one assessment.
  • This is a detailed assignment. Use your time wisely. There is a lot of ground to cover here and you want to make sure you save time for commenting and discussion. In particular, don't use up all of your time reading the chapters if you think you will be short on time. This assignment should not require more than the required 15 hours. Unless you are quite knowledgeable about this material you will certainly need at least ten hours.

1. Read/review the Performance Assessment chapter. Pay close attention to What is a Performance Test. Identifying Suitable Tasks. You really need to grapple with the Generalizability Dilemma before making assessments in this assignment. Be sure to review the Pondertime questions.

2. Read/review the Portfolio Assessment Chapter. Pay close attention to the Classroom vs. Large Scale and Key Ingredients. Be sure to review the Pondertime questions.

State the advantages and disadvantages of performance assessment and then portfolio assessment (a) in general, (b) from the perspective of a teacher, administrator, or researcher, (c) relative to your domain of learning, and (d) relative to your lesson. This does not have to be exhaustive, but make sure you hit the high points. It should be clear why you choose to do one or the other formats for your assignment. Make sure you hit the relevant Pondertime questions for both chapters.

If your original aim does not lend itself to performance assessment, extend or expand it so that it does. But If the alignment is a bit off, don’t stress out. You are expected to reread the “What teachers need to know” section and chapter summaries as you go, and you wikifolio should show explicit evidence of this (e.g., quotes). You may revise your task in light of the criteria as you go but it is not necessary. But you should certainly describe how it might be changed to better reflect the criteria. D. MOST RELEVANT CRITERIA. Indicate which two of the seven evaluative criteria seems most important to you and why. This section should clearly articulate an understanding of how your task, discipline, and role impact your opinions about performance assessment. Depending on your domain, performance assessment may be inevitable (e.g., in composition), intriguing (e.g., social studies), or impossible (e.g., mathematics). Depending on your role, performance assessment can be appealing (e.g., teachers and many educational researcher), aggravating (administrators and parents), or even appalling (e.g., policy makers and psychometricians).

Yes, I am trying to be cute here and I hope it is working. But you should get the point. 4. PORTFOLIO ASSESSMENT. If you elect to do the portfolio assessment exercise, provide an introduction and elaborate on why you choose it. Then do each of the following.

Indicate which two of the five rules seem most important to YOU and why. Again, your domain and role should be articulated here as relevant. D. IMPACT ON TEACHING. How would you expect this portfolio assessment system to impact the way you teach? What evidence might you use to convince a skeptic of these advantages?
