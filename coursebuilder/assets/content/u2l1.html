Selected Response Formats

In this assignment, you will learn some general "commandments" for writing test items and then learn guidelines for specific selected response formats.  Keep in mind, you are not necessarily making assessments that will be used; you are using the context of your lesson to learn these commandments and guidelines.  You will do so in part by figuring out which commandments, selected-response formats, and guidelines are most relevant to you and to your lesson.  Some notes and suggestions:

  1. Before completing this assignment you are encouraged to review all of the Assignment FAQs.
  2. This assignment will have fewer specific steps than the previous assignments; If you forget how to do something you might wish to refer to the more detailed instructions on the previous assignment.
  3. Before you start this assignment, you may wish to go back and complete the reflections and review and respond to comments on the previous assignment.  The reflections for the previous assignment need to be completed by the deadline for the wikifolio for this assignment.
  4. You are responsible for being familiar with the guidelines for all of the item formats, including the ones you did not make items for.

Institutional Role and Lesson

Provide a short summary of your lesson and context from the first assignment for the benefit of your classmates and the instructor (so they don’t have to look back).  You should also add any new insights about the lesson that have emerged as you have thought about it more in the previous assignment(s).

The Task

Create two selected-response items.  Read about the four item types (Binary-Choice Items, Multiple Binary-Choice Items, Multiple-Choice Items, Matching Items). Choose the two items that are the most useful and relevant to your role and lesson.  Make examples for the two items types you chose. Your main goal is to attempt to apply the General Item-Writing Commandments and the specific Item-Writing Guidelines for each of the item types.

Under each of your two items, write the specific Item-Writing Guideline that was most relevant to your construction of that item and briefly explain why that Guideline was most relevant (you may find the guidelines on the next page of this unit).

You may struggle a bit with formatting this part of your assignment.  You may find the table tool helpful for formatting.  Don’t try to make it look too pretty if you are having problems.

Ranking

Ranking of General Item-Writing Guidelines

Popham lists five General Item-Writing Commandments. Figure out which are the most relevant to you and your context and which are the least relevant to you.  Rank order them using the drag and drop tool on your Selected-Response Wikifolio page. (You may wish to review the FAQ on relevance).

Five General Item-Writing Commandments (from Popham, page xxx)

  1. Thou shall not provide opaque directions to students regarding how to respond to your assessment instruments.
  2. Thou shall not emply ambiguous statements in your assessment items.
  3. Though shall not proide students with unintentional clues regarding appropriate responses.
  4. Thou shall not employ complex syntax in your assessment items.
  5. Thou shall not use vocabulary that is more advanced than required.

Reflections

(Due within one week of the original deadline).  In order to receive credit for each wikifolio, you must post three brief reflections at the bottom of the wikifolio.  You must have a coherent reflection that demonstrate understanding of the concepts and how they related to your lesson context.

Consequences.  What are the actual or potential consequences of the ideas in this chapter for your educational practice?  Can you think of anything you might do differently now?  Emphasize things that you learned about assessment and about your lesson.  Are the consequences different because of your current/future role?  

Critiques.  Now that you have a better understanding of these ideas about assessment, how good was your lesson, setting, and role for learning these things?  Did one of your classmates have a better context for learning some of these concepts?  Why?  Note that you are NOT being asked to critique or improve your lesson or your assessments. You are being asked to consider whether your lesson was the ideal context for applying and learning the big ideas in the chapter.

Collaboration.  Whose comments and examples were particularly helpful for your completing this assignment?  Be specific and name people and what they contributed that was helpful.

You are advised to begin drafting the reflections while you are working on the wikifolio, and then to complete them before starting the next assignment.  They should serve as a review and help you better connect the ideas of the chapter to your practice.
