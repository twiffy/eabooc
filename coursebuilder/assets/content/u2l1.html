Assignment 2: Selected-Response Formats

In this assignment, you will create selected-response items for your curricular aim. You will also learn and rank five "commandments" for writing test items in general and the specific guidelines for four common selected-response formats.  Consider the following:

 

  • Remember: The steps in CAPITALS correspond with sections of your wikifolio.   Make headers for each of these sections.
  • You are not necessarily making items you will actually use.  Rather you are creating items to learn about the commandments, formats, and guidelines.  You will do so by figuring out which more relevant to your role and aim.
  • You are expected to become familiar with the items and guidelines for all four item formats, even though you are only required to make items for two (the second two are optional elements).
  • Before you begin you may wish to complete should revisit the previous assignment.  Respond to comments & questions and complete your endorsements, promotions, & reflections. These are due Sunday at 8PM EST/GMT-5, the same time as the draft of this assignment.
  • The words in the heading in CAPITALS correspond directly to headings you should use to organize your wikifolio.

(Based on Popham Chapter 6).

 

Context

1. CONTEXT. Restate your curricular aim and role from the first assignment.  This is for the benefit of the classmates and instructors (to give them context). This is also for your benefit because new insights will come to you each week and you are expected to add them in as your understanding of your aim and role evolve alongside your growing knowledge of assessment.

 

Concepts

2. Rank the FOUR SELECTED-RESPONSE FORMATS.  

Read about the four selected-response item types (Binary-Choice Items, Multiple Binary-Choice Items, Multiple-Choice Items, Matching Items). Rank the four item types according to relevance to your role and context. That is, rank them from which format is the most relevant to you in your role and context to which format is the least relevant to you in your role and context. Be sure to explain your ranking.

 

3. Create TWO SELECTED RESPONSE ITEMS (MOST RELEVANT ITEMS)

Make examples for the two items that you ranked as most relevant to your role and context. Your main goal is to attempt to apply the General Item-Writing Commandments (found under Ranking below) and the specific Item-Writing Guidelines for each of the item types (found on the next page).

 

Under each of your two items, rank the specific Item-Writing Guidelines from most relevant to least relevant in terms of how the guidelines influenced your construction of that item. Write a brief explanation for the most relevant guideline.

 

You may struggle a bit with formatting this part of your assignment.  You may find the table tool helpful for formatting.  Don’t try to make it look too pretty if you are having problems.

4. Create TWO SELECTED RESPONSE ITEMS (LEAST RELEVANT ITEMS)(Optional)

Make examples for the two items that you ranked as least relevant to your role and context. Your main goal is to attempt to apply the General Item-Writing Commandments (found under Ranking below) and the specific Item-Writing Guidelines for each of the item types (found on the next page).

 

Under each of your two items, rank the specific Item-Writing Guidelines from most relevant to least relevant in terms of how the guidelines influenced your construction of that item. Write a brief explanation for the least relevant guideline.

5. Apply GENERAL ITEM-WRITING COMMANDMENTS.

Popham lists five General Item-Writing Commandments (page 156). Figure out which are the most relevant to you and your context and which are the least relevant to you.

 

●     Thou shall not provide opaque directions to students regarding how to respond to your assessment instruments.

●     Thou shall not employ ambiguous statements in your assessment items.

●     Thou shall not provide students with unintentional clues regarding appropriate responses.

●     Thou shall not employ complex syntax in your assessment items.

●     Thou shall not use vocabulary that is more advanced than required.

 

Add brief justifications for the General Item-Writing Commandments that you chose as most relevant and the one you chose as least relevant, keeping in mind your role and context.

 

Big Ideas

6.  Describe three relevant BIG IDEAS about selected-response items. Write a brief summary/explanation where you make it clear why they are the most relevant to you. Try to start with biggest idea and work down. These are the BIG ideas of the chapter not the more specific concepts listed above. You should refer to the What Teacher Need to Know…. section and the summary at the end of Chapter 6. Try to insert your experience and academic domain into this discussion.

 

Self-Check (Optional)

7.  Take the SELF-CHECK.  Answer questions 1-4 without looking up the answers. If you can't answer the items you should go back over that section. Add an an entry in your wikifolio saying which ones you got wrong and why..

Pondertime (Optional)

8. Ponder the PONDERTIME questions.  Do so in light of your role and and aspirations and identify the question that is most relevant to you.  Add an entry for the most relevant question and state the question and explain why it is most relevant.

Interaction (Required)

9. Question, Discuss, ENDORSE, AND PROMOTE. (After the first deadline). After drafts are up, go in and look at your classmates wikifolios and leave comment or suggestions at the bottom.  Each week after completing your draft you should:

A. Question.  Post at least one question to your classmates that this assignment raised for you to help get discussion started.

B. Discuss.  Engage in threaded discussions (see Discussion FAQ)

C. ENDORSE.  While you are commenting, you should endorse at least three of your classmates wikifolios for being complete.  Simply post a comment starting with one ampersand (&) for completing only the required parts and and two ampersands (&&) for completing optional parts as well. (Reflections don’t count; see the Endorsement FAQ)

D. PROMOTE.  After reviewing and discussing, promote one (and only one) wikifolio for being an exemplary effort from your perspective.  Post a comment starting with three ampersands (&&&) and indicate what was exemplary about it.  (see the Promotion FAQ)

Insert an entry in your wikifolio indicating whose wikifolios you endorsed as being complete and whose wikifolio you promoted as being exemplary.

Reflection (Optional)

10. Reflect on your engagement (within one week of the original deadline).  In order to receive credit for this wikifolio, you must post three brief reflections at the bottom of the wikifolio.  You must have a coherent reflection that demonstrate understanding of the concepts and how they related to your curricular aims and context.

A. CONSEQUENTIAL ENGAGEMENT. What might you do differently after reading about this week’s topic and completing this assignment? (see Consequences FAQ)

B. CRITICAL ENGAGEMENT. How well-suited was your role and curricular aim to learning this week’s concepts and big ideas?  Did one of your classmates have a better context for learning some of these concepts?  Note that you are NOT being asked to critique how well you did.  Rather you are reflecting on the fit between your aim and role and the concepts we learned this week. (see Critiques FAQ)

C. COLLABORATIVE ENGAGEMENT. Whose comments and examples were particularly helpful for your completing this assignment?  Be specific and name people and what they contributed that was helpful.

You are advised to begin drafting the reflections while you are working on the wikifolio, and then to complete them before starting the next assignment.  They should serve as a review and help you better connect the ideas of the chapter to your practice.
