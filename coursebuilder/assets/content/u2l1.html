Selected Response Formats

Draft to be Posted by Sunday night at 8 PM

In this assignment, you will learn some general "commandments" for writing test items and then learn guidelines for specific selected response formats. Remember, you are not necessarily making assessments that will be used; you are using the context of your lesson to learn these commandments and guidelines. You will do so in part by figuring out which commandments, selected-response formats, and guidelines are most relevant to you and to your lesson. Some notes and suggestions:

  • Before completing this assignment you are encouraged to review all of the Assignment FAQs.
  • This assignment will have fewer specific steps than the previous assignments; If you forget how to do something you might wish to refer to the more detailed instructions on the previous assignment.
  • Before you start this assignment, you may wish to go back and complete the reflections and review and respond to comments on the previous assignment. The reflections for the previous assignment need to be completed by the deadline for the wikifolio for this assignment.
  • You are responsible for being familiar with the guidelines for all of the item formats, including the ones you did not make items for.

1. Read/Review the chapter. The chapter on selected response items is quite readable but you may find the guidelines hard to make sense of without applying them to your context. Please note—because students may be using different versions of the text, page numbers are not included. Make sure you review the chapter well enough to locate the relevant sections. Be sure to review the Pondertime questions. If you do not address those issues during your assignment you will not be prepared for the exams.

2. Create a Selected Response subpage. Go to your wikifolio home page on the left. Unless one was created for you, create a new page on your wikifolio by (a) clicking the blue New Page icon on the upper right. Then (b) name the page Selected Response, (c) use the default Web Page template, (d) select the location under your wikifolio homepage (your name), and (e) click the red Create button on the upper left. Then save the page. Leave your wikifolio subpage at the bottom of the wikifolio while you are still working on it.

3. Summarize your lesson and context. Provide a short summary of your lesson and context from the first assignment for the benefit of your classmates and the instructor (so they don’t have to look back). You should also add any new insights about the lesson that have emerged as you have thought about it more in the previous assignment(s).

4. Rank the relevance of the general item writing commandments. Study the general item writing commandments and figure out which is most relevant to you and your context and which is least relevant to you. Rank order them and provide a brief summary of what each means and how they are relevant. Make it clear why the first are most relevant (you may wish to review the FAQ on relevance).

5. Create two selected-response items. Read about the four item types and decide which is most useful and relevant to you. Make examples of two of the four. Obviously the items should be related to your instructional activity and your standards, but it does not have to be perfectly aligned. Remember that the main goal is just trying to apply the general item writing commandments and the specific guidelines for each of the item types.

You may struggle a bit with formatting this part of your assignment. You may find the table tool helpful for formatting. Don’t try to make it look too pretty if you are having problems. You may also find it helpful to use the Snipping Tool (in windows) to copy and paste a screen capture image of something you created in a different program.

6. Rank the relevance of the specific item-writing guidelines. For each of the two items you created, determine which of the guidelines for that format were most relevant for you. Summarize each of the guidelines in a way that shows their relevance and why the first ones are most relevant (this might be a good time to insert a comment that is a question to your readers about something that is unclear). Study and consider the relevance of the guidelines for the formats you did not select. You do not need to rank them on your wiki but you are expected to be familiar with them and they will appear on the exam.

7. Summarize three or more relevant big ideas about selected-response items. Review the What Teacher Need to Know…. section and the chapter summary at the end. List three or more big ideas that are most relevant to you. Your summary of the ideas should make it clear why they are the most relevant to you. These are the BIG ideas of the chapter, not the specific guidelines used above.

8. Move up your wikifolio subpage. Once you are ready for your classmates and the instructor to comment, drag and drop a copy of the subpage to underneath Assessment Practices.

9. Discussion (before and after deadline). Post at least one question to the instructor and your classmates, but post it as a comment rather than in the body so readers will see it readily. After drafts are up, go in and look at each others's wikifolios and leave comments or suggestions at the bottom. Be sure to check back and answer any questions or responses. Your goal here is to create extended threaded discussions about the big ideas of the chapter as they relate to you. Try to reference the text and each others activities and comments. The instructor will try to push this conversation along without leading it. The instructor will also provide specific comments to students that should be generally interesting to everyone. Try to divide your time evenly between your group and other groups.

10. Reflections (within one week of the original deadline). In order to receive credit for each wikifolio, you must post three brief reflections at the bottom of the wikifolio. You must have a coherent reflection that demonstrate understanding of the concepts and how they related to your lesson context.

Consequences. What are the actual or potential consequences of the ideas in this chapter for your educational practice? Can you think of anything you might do differently now? Emphasize things that you learned about assessment and about your lesson. Are the consequences different because of your current/future role?

Critiques. Now that you have a better understanding of these ideas about assessment, how good was your lesson, setting, and role for learning these things? Did one of your classmates have a better context for learning some of these concepts? Why? Note that you are NOT being asked to critique or improve your lesson or your assessments. You are being asked to consider whether your lesson was the ideal context for applying and learning the big ideas in the chapter.

Collaboration. Whose comments and examples were particularly helpful for your completing this assignment? Be specific and name people and what they contributed that was helpful.

You are advised to begin drafting the reflections while you are working on the wikifolio, and then completing them before starting the next assignment. They should serve as a review and help you better connect the ideas of the chapter to your practice.

11. Self-Check (Optional). You are encouraged to complete the self-check though you may wish to do so before the section quiz. Take it yourself without looking up the answers. If you can't answer the items you should go back over that section.
