Unit 2: Selected-Response Formats

Overview

In this assignment, you will  learn some "commandments" for writing test items in general and the specific guidelines for four common selected-response formats.  You will do so by creating some constructed response items for your curricular aim. Consider the following:

 

  • In this assignment, you will use most of the key concepts from Chapter 6 in Popham’s text Classroom Assessment: What Teachers Need to Know. To really master these ideas, read the entire chapter in the context of completing the assignment.
  • You are not necessarily making items you will actually use.  Rather you are creating items to learn about the commandments, formats, and guidelines.  You will do so by figuring out which more relevant to your role and aim.
  • You are expected to become familiar with the items and guidelines for all four item formats, even though you are only required to make items for two (the second two are optional elements).

REITERATE YOUR CONTEXT (BY SEPTEMBER 22)

Restate your curricular aim and role from the first assignment.  This is for the benefit of the classmates and instructors (to give them context). This is also for your benefit because new insights about your aim and role will come to you each week.  You are expected to refine your aim and context as your understanding evolves alongside your growing knowledge of assessment.

 

CONSIDER THE CONCEPTS (ALL REQUIRED BY SEPTEMBER 22)

Relevant Selected-Response Formats

Read about the four selected-response item types (Binary-Choice Items, Multiple Binary-Choice Items, Multiple-Choice Items, Matching Items). Rank the four item types according to relevance to your role and context. That is, rank them from which format is the most relevant to you in your role and context to which format is the least relevant to you in your role and context. Do your best to to explain your ranking. If you are unsure, say so and invite your classmates to help you.

 

Create Two Selected-Response Items (Most Relevant Formats)

Create selected response items for your curricular aim using the two item types that you ranked as most relevant to your role and context. Your main goal is to apply the General Item-Writing Commandments and the specific Item-Writing Guidelines for each of the selected-response item types (the commandments and guidelines are summarized below and detailed in Chapter 6).

 

Under each of your two items, rank the specific Item-Writing Guidelines from most relevant to least relevant in terms of how the guidelines influenced your construction of that item. Write a brief explanation for the most relevant guideline.  You may wish to reference the Assignment FAQ on Relevance.

 

You may struggle a bit with formatting this part of your assignment.  You may find the table tool helpful for formatting.  Don’t try to make it look too pretty if you are having problems.

Apply General Item-Writing Commandments

Popham lists five General Item-Writing Commandments (page 156). Figure out which are the most relevant to you and your context and which are the least relevant to you.

 

●     Thou shall not provide opaque directions to students regarding how to respond to your assessment instruments.

●     Thou shall not employ ambiguous statements in your assessment items.

●     Thou shall not provide students with unintentional clues regarding appropriate responses.

●     Thou shall not employ complex syntax in your assessment items.

●     Thou shall not use vocabulary that is more advanced than required.

 

Add brief justifications for the General Item-Writing Commandments that you chose as most relevant and the one you chose as least relevant, keeping in mind your aim, role and context.

 

Big Ideas

Review the chapter and assignment and read the Chapter 6 Summary and What Teachers Need to Know.  Come up with three big ideas about selected-response formats.  Number the ideas (1, 2, 3) and list them in order of importance to you, keeping in mind your role and context. Write a brief summary/explanation of each idea where you make it clear why they are relevant to you. These are the BIG ideas of the chapter not the more specific concepts listed above. Try to insert your experience and academic domain into this discussion.

Reflection (by September 29)

Reflect on your engagement (within one week of the original deadline).  Post three brief reflections. You should write a coherent reflection that demonstrates understanding of the concepts and how they related to your curricular aims and context. On your Wikifolio, be sure to label each of the three types of engagement as you present your reflection.

A. Consequential Engagement. What might you do differently after reading about this week’s topic and completing this assignment?

B. Critical Engagement. How well-suited was your role and curricular aim to learning this week’s concepts and big ideas?  Did one of your classmates have a better context for learning some of these concepts?  Note that you are NOT being asked to critique how well you did.  Rather you are reflecting on the fit between your aim and role and the concepts we learned this week.

C. Collaborative Engagement. Whose comments and examples were particularly helpful for your completing this assignment?  Be specific and name people and what they contributed that was helpful.

You are advised to begin drafting the reflections while you are working on the Wikifolio, and then to complete them before starting the next assignment.  They should serve as a review and help you better connect the ideas of the chapter to your practice.
