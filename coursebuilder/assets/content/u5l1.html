RELIABILITY AND BIAS WIKIFOLIO

In this assignment you will learn about reliability and bias by considering the relevance of the ideas in those chapters for your assessments and your role. These are both short chapters but they discuss some complex concepts.

There are no right or wrong answers. If you still can't come up with anything to say after reading your classmates entries, then you are free to indicate that in your entries. These are hard questions, and some of you will struggle more than others. But you will return to these issues throughout the semester.

1. LESSON & ROLE. Start a new subpage called Reliability and Bias and summarize your assessment practice (the suite of assessments you have developed) and your institutional role as they relate to reliability and bias. Highlight the aspects of your lesson and role that are particularly relevant to these issues. You will certainly need to revise these after you have completed the rest of the assignment.

RELIABILITY
2. Read/Review Chapter 3. Read the introduction carefully and read the Pondertime questions to shape your assignment. Review the different types of reliability and the standard error of measurement.

3. RELEVANCE OF RELIABILITY. Summarize the three types of reliability evidence and rank them in order of relevance in general for you and your given goal. You need to study them well enough to articulate why the first one is most relevant and why the last one is least relevant. You can borrow ideas from your classmates but not actual text. Your work should address Pondertime questions 1, 3, and 4.

4. ASSESSMENT REVISIONS. For each of your examples of the following assessments, state which type of reliability evidence seems most relevant. Then state what you might do to increase the reliability of YOUR assessments. This must be specific. Look long and hard at each of your assessments in light of the ideas in the chapter.

A. Selected-Response Items
B. Constructed-Response Items
C. Essay Item
C. Performance or Portfolio

5. STANDARD ERROR OF MEASUREMENT. Use your assessment practice as a context to explain what the SEM is and why it is such an important concept in educational measurement.

6. BIG IDEAS ABOUT RELIABILITY. Read the What Teachers need to Know and Chapter Summary, identify and make a summary of three big ideas in reliability, starting with the biggest. Try to to insert your role and academic domain into this discussion. Consider starting with "As a _____ who mostly teaches ____....."

ABSENCE OF BIAS

7. Read/Review Chapter 5. Read the introduction and the Pondertime carefully. If you work with English Language Learners or students with learning disabilities and/or are in the administrator's group you need to read those sections carefully. Note that each paragraph represents a “big idea.” If you are struggling, list what the big ideas are. You may find it easier to read the sections carefully in the context of the assignment. You may also wish to watch this video from the author.

8. BIAS RISKS AND REDUCTION. For each of the following assessment formats, write a paragraph that discusses (a) which type of bias (offensiveness versus unfair penalization) seems like the biggest risk, which approach to reducing bias (panel judgement, per item judgement, overall judgement, or empirical) seems most appropriate. Then state what you could to do decrease bias. You must be specific here and if you can''t think of any way might modify it than you need to say so.

A. Selected-Response Items
B. Constructed-Response Items
C. Essay Assessment
D. Performance Assessment
E. Portfolio Assessment

9. BIG IDEAS ABOUT REDUCING BIAS. Read the What Teachers need to Know and Chapter Summary and identify and summarize the three biggest ideas in reducing bias, starting with the biggest. Be sure to project your role and possibly your academic domain into this discussion.

10. Move up your wikifolio subpage. Once you are ready for your classmates and the instructor to comment, drag and drop a copy of the subpage up under the appropriate section so your classmates and the instructor know it is ready to be discussed.

11. Discussion (before and after deadline). Post at least one question to the instructor and your classmates, but post it as a comment rather than in the body so readers will see it readily. After drafts are up, go in and look at each others wikifolios and leave comment or suggestions at the bottom. Be sure to check back and answer any questions or responses. Your goal here is to create extended threaded discussions about the big ideas of the chapter as they relate to you. Try to reference the text and each others activities and comments. The instructor will try to push this conversation along without leading it. The instructor will also provide specific comments to students that should be generally interesting to everyone. Try to divide your time evenly between your group and other groups.

12. REFLECTIONS (within one week of the original deadline). In order to receive credit for each wikifolio, you must post three brief reflections at the bottom of the wikifolio. You must have a coherent reflection that demonstrate understanding of the concepts and how they related to your lesson context.

Consequences. What are the actual or potential consequences of learning the ideas in this chapter for your educational practice? Can you think of anything you might do differently after reading this chapter and completing this assignment? Emphasize things that you learned about assessment and about your lesson from reading and discussing the chapter.

Critiques. Now that you have a better understanding of these ideas about assessment, how good was your lesson, setting, and role for learning these things? Did one of your classmates have a better context for learning some of these concepts? Why? Note that you are NOT being asked to critique or improve your lesson or your assessments. You are being asked to consider the appropriateness of your lesson as a context for applying and learning the big ideas in the chapter.

Collaboration. Whose comments and examples were particularly helpful for learning the ideas in this chapter? Be specific and name people and what they contributed that was helpful.

You are advised to begin drafting the reflections while you are working on the wikifolio, and then completing them before starting the next assignment. They should serve as a review and help you better connect the ideas of the chapter to your practice.

13. Self-Check (Optional). You are encouraged to complete the self-check though you may wish to do so before the section quiz. Take it yourself without looking up the answers. If you can't answer the items you should go back over that section.
