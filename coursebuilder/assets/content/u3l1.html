In this assignment, you will make two short answer items, an essay item, and a rubric for the essay item and study the guidelines for each in Chapter 7. There is also an optional activity to create a flawed item. You will also read about curricular aims in Chapter 2

  • Before completing this assignment you are encouraged to review all of the Assignment FAQs.
  • The parts of this assignment will give fewer specific instructions than the previous assignments; if your forget how to do things look at the earlier assignments.
  • Before you start this assignment, you may wish to go back and complete the reflections and review and respond to the previous assignment.
  • The things in CAPITALS correspond with sections (i,.e, headers) in your wikifolio.
1. Read/review Chapter 7, Constructed Response Tests. You can skim the guidelines but read the rest of the chapter carefully. Be sure to review the Pondertime questions so that you address them when completing the wikifolio to be sure you engage with the big ideas.

2. Create a Constructed Response Items subpage. Leave your wikifolio subpage at the bottom of the wikifolio until you are ready for you classmates and the instructor to review and comment on it.

3. LESSON AND CURRICULAR AIM. Read the What to Assess section in Chapter 2 carefully; If you are in a K-12 setting and impacted by state standards you should read that section now; we will return to standards later too. Review the What to Assess Considerations (p. 51 in Seventh Edition) and restate and extend your lesson description to include your curricular aim. Your lesson is about teaching; but your curricular aim is about learning.

4. SHORT ANSWER ITEMS: Read guidelines, write two or more items, rank guidelines. Review the general guidelines in Chapter 6 and read specific guidelines in Chapter 7. Follow the guidelines and make two or more items. Indicate what the correct answer is and what other likely incorrect answers are. Rank order the specific guidelines and provide a brief summary of what each means and how they are relevant to YOU. Make it clear why the first ones are most relevant.

5. ESSAY ITEM: Read guidelines, write an item, rank guidelines. Review the general guidelines in Chapter 6 and read the specific guidelines in Chapter 7. Follow the guidelines and make an essay item that reflects them. Rank order the specific guidelines and provide a brief summary of what each means and how they are relevant to YOU. Make the order of relevance clear.

Note: your lesson might be in a domain like mathematics where an essay item seems impossible. That might be true but you should try to create one. Consider rethinking the goal of your lesson or even restating it so it might fit an essay. For example, you might ask students to explain how an algebra problem is solved in their own words.

6. ESSAY SCORING KEY: Read guidelines, write key, rank guidelines. Review the general guidelines in Chapter 6 and read the specific guidelines in Chapter 7. Make a scoring key that reflects them. Summarize the items in order of relevance to YOU.

7. FLAWED ITEM (Optional). Make a new subpage and create a constructed response item that violates one or more of the guidelines. Ask you classmates to look at the item and comment on what guidelines you violated by putting comments at the bottom. Try to violate as many as you can and see if your classmates can find them.

8. ADVANTAGES AND DISADVANTAGES OF COMMON ITEM FORMATS. Review the Pondertime questions for Chapter 2, 6, and 7, and read the How to Assess it section in Chapter 2. Then summarize the advantages and disadvantages of constructed-response, short answer and essay items. We will return to reliability (consistency of scores), validity (strength of inferences), and bias in the middle of the course but see if you can make sense of those ideas now. Try to consider advantage and disadvantages relative to your role, your learning domain, and your specific lesson.

List three or more big ideas that are most relevant to YOU. These are not specific guidelines but the big ideas we are concerned with.

10. Move up your wikifolio subpage. Once you are ready for your classmates and the instructor to comment, drag and drop a copy of the subpage to underneath Assessment Practices.

11. Discussion (before and after deadline). Post at least one question to the instructor and your classmates, but post it as a comment rather than in the body so readers will see it readily. After drafts are up, go in and look at each others wikifolios and leave comments or suggestions at the bottom. Be sure to check back and answer any questions or responses. Your goal here is to create extended threaded discussions about the big ideas of the chapter as they relate to you. Try to reference the text and each others activities and comments. The instructor will try to push this conversation along without leading it. The instructor will also provide specific comments to students that should be generally interesting to everyone. Try to divide your time evenly between your group and other groups.

12. REFLECTIONS (within one week of the original deadline). In order to receive credit for each wikifolio, you must post three brief reflections at the bottom of the wikifolio. You must have a coherent reflection that demonstrate understanding of the concepts and how they related to your lesson context.

Consequences. What are the actual or potential consequences of learning the ideas in this chapter for your educational practice? Can you think of anything you might do differently after reading this chapter and completing this assignment? Emphasize things that you learned about assessment and about your lesson from reading and discussing the chapter.

Critiques. Now that you have a better understanding of these ideas about assessment, how good was your lesson, setting, and role for learning these things? Did one of your classmates have a better context for learning some of these concepts? Why? Note that you are NOT being asked to critique or improve your lesson or your assessments. You are being asked to consider the appropriateness of your lesson as a context for applying and learning the big ideas in the chapter.

Collaboration. Whose comments and examples were particularly helpful for learning the ideas in this chapter? Be specific and name people and what they contributed that was helpful.

You are advised to begin drafting the reflections while you are working on the wikifolio, and then completing them before starting the next assignment. They should serve as a review and help you better connect the ideas of the chapter to your practice.

13. Self-Check (Optional). You are encourage to complete the self-check though you may wish to do so before the section quiz. Take it yourself without looking up the answers. If you can't answer the items you should go back over that section.
