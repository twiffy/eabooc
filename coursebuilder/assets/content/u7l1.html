FORMATIVE ASSESSMENT

This assignment helps you make sense of the complex relationship between assessment and learning. This chapter of the text has been substantially reworked and reflects a growing appreciation of the formative and transformative potential of assessment for enhancing and changing the way students learn and teachers teach.

For many of you, this chapter and assignment may have profound consequences for your teaching practice. This is a detailed assignment so use your time wisely and make sure that you save time for reading and discussing the work of other. Please remember that there is a wide range of ambitions and experience in this class. Some students are likely to really dive into this assignment. If you run out of time to do as good of a job as you want to on your wikifolio, do as much as you can and say so. But do enough so that you can reflect on it and make sense of the work of your classmates.

1. LESSON & ROLE. Start a new subpage called Formative Assessment and summarize your assessment practice (the suite of assessments you have developed) and your institutional role as they relate to formative assessment. Highlight the aspects of your lesson and role that are particularly relevant to these issues. You will certainly have to return to this after you have worked on the lesson for some time.

2. Read/Review Chapter 12. Read the chapter through Learning Progressions very carefully. Reread it if you have to. By this point in the course, all of you should have enough experience and practice with assessment to be able to make sense of these complex concepts. Read the introduction carefully and read the Pondertime questions to shape your assignment. Review the different types of validity evidence as well.

3. DEFINE FORMATIVE ASSESSMENT. The chapter does a nice job summarizing the complex and controversial issues around formative assessment. It means very different things to different people. There are numerous opinions about formative assessment out there. Your instructor has been deeply involved in this issue for much of his career. A recent readable summary of the instructor's work in this regard is contained in the attached Chapter. You can see where the disagreement with Popham comes from. Here are some other resources:

Rick Wormeli: Formative and Summative Assessment
Dylan Wiliam: Embedded Formative Assessment
Formative Assessment in a Primary Classroom | KET
Measured Progress: The Formative Classroom
Popham highlights how the term formative assessment has been exploited by commercial firms that are essentially selling drill and practice test preparation materials. These practices raise serious issues concerning evidential and consequential validity. As a developing assessment expert, you should have some well-formed opinions on this topic that reflect your role in education and the domain you teach in. You should write at least four paragraphs that summarizes your stance on formative assessment. You likely will want to start this at the beginning of this assignment and then complete it after finishing the other activities. Be sure to address Pondertime 3 and 4.

4. OUTLINE A LEARNING PROGRESSION. Carefully read the section on Learning Progressions. Then describe what the learning progression might look like for your lesson, with as much detail as you can manage given the time you have available.

A. CURRICULAR AIM. Reiterate your curricular aim from your prior assignments.
B. BUILDING BLOCKS. Identify at least three precursory subskills and/or elements of enabling knowledge.
C. DETERMINE MEASURABILITY. Determine the measurability of the building blocks.
D. ARRANGE THE BUILDING BLOCKS. Describe how the learning progression might be used in class.

9. REFLECTIONS (within one week of the original deadline). In order to receive credit for each wikifolio, you must post three brief reflections at the bottom of the wikifolio. You must have a coherent reflection that demonstrates understanding of the concepts and how they are related to your lesson context.

Consequences. What are the actual or potential consequences of learning the ideas in this chapter for your educational practice? Can you think of anything you might do differently after reading this chapter and completing this assignment? Emphasize things that you learned about assessment and about your lesson from reading and discussing the chapter.

Critiques. Now that you have a better understanding of these ideas about assessment, how good was your lesson, setting, and role for learning these things? Did one of your classmates have a better context for learning some of these concepts? Why? Note that you are NOT being asked to critique or improve your lesson or your assessments. You are being asked to consider the appropriateness of your lesson as a context for applying and learning the big ideas in the chapter.

Collaboration. Whose comments and examples were particularly helpful for learning the ideas in this chapter? Be specific and name people and what they contributed that was helpful.

You are advised to begin drafting the reflections while you are working on the wikifolio, and then completing them before starting the next assignment. They should serve as a review and help you better connect the ideas of the chapter to your practice.
