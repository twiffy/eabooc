TEST PREPARATION

In this assignment, you will consider appropriate and inappropriate test preparation practices by drafting scenarios like the chapter self-check, and discussing them with your classmates to see whether or not they are appropriate. Be sure to read the chapter and complete the self-checks before beginning this assignment.

To help appreciate how these guidelines apply to broader testing formats, you are asked to create scenarios for the high-stakes assessments your students will face. This is the point in the course where everybody should be confidently projecting their professional identity and recognizing and anticipating those of their classmates, What you teach and where you teach it makes a very big difference in what is appropriate. Likewise, there is often a wide divergence between what administrators and teachers think is appropriate. There should be some serious discussion here about these issues.

1. Read/Review Chapter 14. Read the chapter, and make sure you have a sense of the two evaluative guidelines, the five test prep practices, and the nuances of teaching to the test. It is a short chapter so you should read it straight through, while considering how these things mean different things for different assessment formats, domains, and roles.

2. INSTITUTIONAL ROLE. Make a subpage and summarize your current and/or future role in the educational ecosystem relative test preparation. What is appropriate and inappropriate may depend on your role and some subjects and tests are more readily compromised. Those of you in non-school settings have a choice of getting creative and finding something like a workplace test or something, or just using one of the K-12 tests.

3. GENERAL TEST PREPARATION GUIDELINES. Summarize the two evaluative guidelines in your own words. Try to elaborate on the consequences of these guidelines for practice, consider the challenges educators might encounter in following these guidelines, and summarize the role that test form and test item format play in distinguishing between appropriate and inappropriate test preparation.

Explicitly ponder how these guidelines take on different meanings for (a) different assessment formats, (b) different domains, and (c) different roles. If you are a future administrator, try to write in the imperative or whatever way seems appropriate.

4. SELECTED-RESPONSE SCENARIO. Draft a scenario based on your own teaching and testing context that requires a classmate to apply two guidelines relative to preparing your students for the highest-stakes test they face. You scenario must include a reference to item format (i.e., require a judgment as to whether the format is same or varied); try to include another test preparation practice in your scenario as well. Try to draft a tricky scenario that prompts discussion and debate, and reveals how different stakeholders have different interpretations of what is “ethical” and “educational.” Post a comment to your classmates asking if your scenario is appropriate or not.

5. CONSTRUCTED-RESPONSE/PERFORMANCE/PORTFOLIO SCENARIO. Create another new page on your wiki. Imagine that your students will be facing high-stakes test with the one of these formats that is most relevant to you. You can use the items you created or use any other example that will help make for a productive exercise. Again, try to draft a tricky scenario that helps reveal the range of interpretations of “ethical” and “educational” and how this is complicated by broader item formats. Post a comment to your classmates asking if your scenario was is appropriate or not.

6. BIG IDEAS ABOUT TEST PREPARATION. Review What to Classroom Teachers Need to Know and the Summary. State the three most relevant big ideas about standardized testing for you and people in your role. Make it clear why the first one is most relevant.

7. Move up your wikifolio subpage. Once you are ready for your classmates and the instructor to comment, drag and drop a copy of the subpage up under the appropriate section so your classmates and the instructor know it is ready to be discussed

8. Discussion (before and after deadline). Post at least one question to the instructor and your classmates, but post it as a comment rather than in the body so readers will see it readily. After drafts are up, go in and look at each others's wikifolios and leave comment or suggestions at the bottom. Be sure to check back and answer any questions or responses.

9. REFLECTIONS (within one week of the original deadline). In order to receive credit for each wikifolio, you must post three brief reflections at the bottom of the wikifolio. You must have a coherent reflection that demonstrate understanding of the concepts and how they related to your lesson context.

Consequences. What are the actual or potential consequences of learning the ideas in this chapter for your educational practice? Can you think of anything you might do differently after reading this chapter and completing this assignment? Emphasize things that you learned about assessment and about your lesson from reading and discussing the chapter.

Did one of your classmates have a better context for learning some of these concepts? Why? Note that you are NOT being asked to critique or improve your lesson or your assessments. You are being asked to consider the appropriateness of your lesson as a context for applying and learning the big ideas in the chapter.
