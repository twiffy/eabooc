Goal-Attainment Grading Scenario

Begin by carefully reading this article by Roach and Elliot (2006) on Goal Attainment Scaling. While this article was written for one particular population of students, the explanation of goal-attainment grading is very clear and applicable in most settings. This article is a good example of how every educator can benefit from the strategies that have proven effective for working with students who are struggling to keep up with their classmates.  Your scenario should work in all of the grade-giving specifics listed in the first optional element below.

Then, describe how you would carry out goal-attainment grading for your curricular aim by doing each of the following:

A. Create a Scenario

Write a 1-2 paragraph scenario about a student in your class that would benefit from goal-attainment grading (refer to Roach and Elliot’s article, page 11, for a sample). Be sure the scenario focuses on the student’s (lack of) progress toward your curricular aim.

B. Identify Goal-Attainment Evidence

Describe what you will use as goal-attainment evidence with this student (Roach and Elliot calls this “target behavior”). That is, you should describe the evidence you will collect to show that the student is making progress toward the curricular aim.

C. Create a Goal-Attainment Scale

Develop a scale, much like Roach and Elliot’s scale (page 10), to show how you might measure the student’s progress toward the goal. Feel free to use other scales that you might have used or learned about in the past.

D. Weight Goal-Attainment Evidence

Describe how much weight the goal-attainment evidence will have in relation to other assignments/assessments/etc. Be prepared to explain why this particular goal-attainment evidence is weighted more or less than other assignments/assessments/etc in the course. Also, explain how you arrive at the final grade for the course, showing your peers how this one piece of goal-attainment evidence described above fits into the entire course.

Reflection (By November 28)

Post three brief reflections. You should write a coherent reflection that demonstrates understanding of the concepts and how they related to your curricular aims and context. On your Wikifolio, be sure to label each of the three types of engagement as you present your reflection.

  1. Consequential Engagement. What might you do differently after reading about this week’s topic and completing this assignment?
  2. Critical Engagement. How well-suited was your role and curricular aim to learning this week’s concepts and big ideas?  Did one of your classmates have a better context for learning some of these concepts?  Note that you are NOT being asked to critique how well you did.  Rather you are reflecting on the fit between your aim and role and the concepts we learned this week.
  3. Collaborative Engagement. Whose comments and examples were particularly helpful for your completing this assignment?  Be specific and name people and what they contributed that was helpful.

You are advised to begin drafting the reflections while you are working on the Wikifolio, and then to complete them before starting the next assignment.  They should serve as a review and help you better connect the ideas of the chapter to your practice.
