Overview

In this assignment, you will learn about appropriate and inappropriate test preparation practices by drafting scenarios and discussing them with your classmates to see whether or not they are appropriate.

To help appreciate how these guidelines apply to different testing formats, you are asked to create scenarios for different high-stakes assessments your students will face. This is the point in the course where everybody should be confidently projecting their professional identity and recognizing and anticipating those of their classmates.  What you teach, where you teach, and how students are assessed makes a very big difference in what is appropriate. Likewise, there is often a wide divergence between what administrators and teachers think is appropriate. There should be some serious discussion here about these issues.

This assignment is associated with Popham Chapter 14 (7th edition).  There are chapters in this topic in the earlier versions and there is quite a bit of information on this topic available on the web.

Consider The Concepts (All required by July 13)

General Test Preparation Guidelines

There are two guidelines that can be used to judge whether an assessment is “appropriate” or “inappropriate,” says Popham. These two guidelines are:

  • Professional Ethics -- “No test-preparation should violate the ethical norms of the education profession” (Popham, p. 358).
  • Educational Defensibility -- “No test-preparation practice should increase students’ test scores without simultaneously increasing students’ mastery of the curricular aim tested” (Popham, p. 359).

In a number of paragraphs, explain and discuss the two evaluative guidelines in your own words. Try to elaborate on the consequences of these guidelines for practice, consider the challenges educators might encounter in following these guidelines, and summarize the role that test form and test item format play in distinguishing between appropriate and inappropriate test preparation.

Explicitly ponder how these guidelines take on different meanings for (a) different assessment formats, (b) different domains, and (c) different roles.

Apply Educationally Defensible Test Preparation Practices

Popham describes five Test-Preparation Practices that are very commonly used among teachers. He finds that only two of the five satisfy both Professional Ethics and Educational Defensibility (table 14.1, page 364):.

  • Varied format preparation involves preparing students with classroom assessments that represent content in varied ways, which can include the way it is represented on the test.
  • Generalized test taking preparation involves special instructions for particular types of test formats.

Rearrange the boxes to indicate which form of preparation is most appropriate for whatever high-stakes tests your students face.  Even if you accept the order that they are, move them up and down so the assignment knows you did the activity. Then explain why the first is more relevant than the second.  You can use any criteria that are important to you, but your answers should show understanding of the difference between increased scores and increased learning.

Consider Inappropriateness of Common Test Preparation Practices

Popham describes three common test preparation practices that are not educationally defensible:

  • Previous-form preparation provides special instruction and practice using previous forms of the actual test
  • Current-form preparation provides special instruction and practice using the test currently being used
  • Same format preparation uses practice items that are identical in format to the items on the targeted test, even though the content of the items may be different.

Rearrange to boxes to indicate which form of preparation is most inappropriate in terms of inflating scores, undermining learning, etc.  In other words the first form or preparation is the one that is most damaging to students, while the last one is least damaging.  (rearrange the boxes even if you accept the order they start in).  Then provide a justification for the ranking that makes it clear why the first one is most inappropriate and the last one is least inappropriate.  Take into account the overall consequences of the practice and consider discussion how common these practices are and how hard it is to avoid them.
