Overview

In this assignment you will learn about whatever standardized test is most relevant to you and how to interpret these tests. Many of you will explore issues related to the controversial new test coming to US schools. You will also want to read and discuss the issues that are articulated in Pophams’ chapter on Standardized Tests.  Reminder: You are all required to post a question as a comment to your classmates.  This is not an optional part of the assignment.  Try to post an open ended question about your wikifolio that gets discussion started.

A note about standardized tests: This assignment will present the K-12 standardized tests for Indiana, Illinois, and Georgia as well as a couple of standardized tests from ETS. If you live in another state or if there is another test that is relevant to your role/context and it has some sort of interpretation guide, then use that test for this assignment. Those of you in non-school settings have a choice of getting creative and finding something like a personality test or another sort of aptitude test, or just using one of the K-12 tests. You may have to modify the assignment a bit to suit you. Look over the interpretation guide and the assignment and if it still does not make sense contact the teaching assistant.

If you do not work with any standardized tests then just complete the assignment with one of the ones provided. This assignment is really about making sense of a handful of crucial concepts that anybody who has some expertise in assessment should be familiar with. Feel free to customize this assignment if needed to cover whatever stuff is relevant to interpreting standardized tests in your context.

For additional information, you may choose to read Popham’s 2001 article “Uses and Misuses of Standardized Tests.”

Reflection (By November 10)

Post three brief reflections. You should write a coherent reflection that demonstrates understanding of the concepts and how they related to your curricular aims and context. On your Wikifolio, be sure to label each of the three types of engagement as you present your reflection.

  1. Consequential Engagement. What might you do differently after reading about this week’s topic and completing this assignment?
  2. Critical Engagement. How well-suited was your role and curricular aim to learning this week’s concepts and big ideas?  Did one of your classmates have a better context for learning some of these concepts?  Note that you are NOT being asked to critique how well you did.  Rather you are reflecting on the fit between your aim and role and the concepts we learned this week.
  3. Collaborative Engagement. Whose comments and examples were particularly helpful for your completing this assignment?  Be specific and name people and what they contributed that was helpful.

You are advised to begin drafting the reflections while you are working on the Wikifolio, and then to complete them before starting the next assignment.  They should serve as a review and help you better connect the ideas of the chapter to your practice.
