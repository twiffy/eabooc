Overview

In this assignment, you will define Formative Assessment, create a Learning Progression, and explore roadblocks to using Formative Assessment. You will make sense of the complex relationship between assessment and learning.  For many of you, this assignment may have profound consequences for your teaching practice or your administrative work.  This is a detailed assignment so use your time wisely and make sure that you save time to read and comment on the work of others.  

This unit is based on Popham Chapter 12 in the 7th edition.  You can complete most of the assignment with the Chapter 12 in the 6th edition but you will need to make some adjustments.

Consider The Concepts (All Required By October 27)

Define Formative Assessment

Formative Assessment means very different things to different people.  There are numerous opinions about formative assessment out there.  Popham, in Chapter 12, does a nice job summarizing the complex and controversial issues around formative assessment. He also discusses Formative Assessment at this link. You might begin to explore how different assumptions about knowing and learning impact what formative assessment looks like.  Here are some other resources you can explore:

Rick Wormeli: Formative and Summative Assessment

Dylan Wiliam: Embedded Formative Assessment

Formative Assessment in a Primary Classroom | KET

Measured Progress: The Formative Classroom

In the textbook, Popham highlights how the term formative assessment has been exploited by commercial firms that are essentially selling drill and practice test preparation materials.  These practices raise serious issues concerning evidential and consequential validity.  As a developing assessment expert, you should have some well-formed opinions on this topic that reflect your role in education and the domain you teach in.

You should write at least two paragraphs (preferably more) that summarize your stance on formative assessment.  You likely will want to start this at the beginning of this assignment and then complete it after finishing the other activities.  Please be sure to cite your information and provide links to any additional information you use to complete this assignment.

Obstacles to Formative Assessment

Discuss the obstacles you might face when implementing your chosen approach to formative assessment. What obstacles might you encounter? Why do you think you would encounter those obstacles? How might you overcome them?  As part of your discussion, rank the three most common roadblocks to Formative Assessment in order of relevance within your role and context (Popham, p. 314-315).

  • Educators have considerable misunderstandings about the nature of formative assessment.
  • Educators are resistant to changing their ways.
  • Despite the fact that formative assessment can improve students’ achievement, and improve it dramatically, this improved achievement may not show up on many external accountability tests.

Add brief justifications for the Roadblocks to Formative Assessment that you chose as most relevant and the one you chose as least relevant, keeping in mind your role and context.

Reflection (By November 3)

Post three brief reflections. You should write a coherent reflection that demonstrates understanding of the concepts and how they related to your curricular aims and context. On your Wikifolio, be sure to label each of the three types of engagement as you present your reflection.

  1. Consequential Engagement. What might you do differently after reading about this week’s topic and completing this assignment?
  2. Critical Engagement. How well-suited was your role and curricular aim to learning this week’s concepts and big ideas?  Did one of your classmates have a better context for learning some of these concepts?  Note that you are NOT being asked to critique how well you did.  Rather you are reflecting on the fit between your aim and role and the concepts we learned this week.
  3. Collaborative Engagement. Whose comments and examples were particularly helpful for your completing this assignment?  Be specific and name people and what they contributed that was helpful.

You are advised to begin drafting the reflections while you are working on the Wikifolio, and then to complete them before starting the next assignment.  They should serve as a review and help you better connect the ideas of the chapter to your practice.
