Assignment 1: Curricular Aim and Standard Selection

In this assignment, you will complete a “wikifolio” on you your homepage.  You will further refine the Curricular Aim that you drafted when you registered and further define the context in which you use educational assessment.  You will then identify an educational standard that is aligned to your Curricular Aim and apply some of the concepts in Chapter Two. After that you will discuss and endorse wikifolios by posting comments. Finally, you will reflect your engagement in this assignment.  Here are some pointers:

  • The steps in CAPITALS correspond with sections of your wikifolio.   Make headers for each of these sections.
  • You should open this assignment in a separate browser window so you can look at it while working (just drag the window out of your browser).  
  • Read the entire assignment before you begin.
  • There are some really important instructions in bold alongside some rather basic procedural stuff. The later assignments will drop much of the procedural stuff..
  • You must post a draft by Sunday night at 8 pm EST (GMT+5) if you want it to be endorsed and get credit for it
  • There are Frequently Asked Questions pages (FAQs) for assignments in general (here) and for this assignment in particular (here)  You can review them first or wait until you get to them.  If you still have questions, post them to appropriate discussion forum
  • Each week, the instructor will provide detailed feedback in the weekly updates. You may want to wait until you see the weekly update to ask questions about the assignment
  • This week you will develop your Context that you will then become the basic context from which you will build the remaining ten assignments in the course. Initially, this will be a large task to complete. In future weeks, you will provide a short summary of what you create this week.

(Based on Popham Chapter 2).

This assignment will likely take you longer than the subsequent assignments. This because it is a foundation for the course and it introduces some new activities.  If you get confused look at the example wikifolio or your classmate’s assignments.

Context

First, open your wikifolio.  Locate your homepage and click on the link for this assignment.  Then click on the blue edit icon on the upper left to open the editing window.

1. Define your ROLE. Describe your current or future role (for instance, Educator, Administrator, Researcher). You will use this role as your lens to the material we cover in this course. It is important that you carefully examine all of the material from the viewpoint of your chosen role - the role you choose (current or future) will greatly impact how you view the material in this course. When you explain your role, be sure to state how you imagine the role will influence your understanding of Educational Assessment.

2. Define your SETTING. Describe the setting in which you plan to take the above role. Consider grade level(s), class size, institution, institution size, governmental/non-profit/ for-profit, etc. Provide enough information so that everyone can understand your setting. You may choose to include a link, or you can describe your setting abstractly (that is, you may keep your actual setting unknown).

3.  Refine your CURRICULAR AIM. Read about curricular aims and what-to-assess considerations in Chapter 2.  Popham describes the “curricular aim” as the desired outcomes of instruction. This is not the “educational objectives,” “instructional objectives,” and “behavioral objectives,” but rather the measurable learning goals. In very simple terms, a Curricular Aim can be viewed as a skill or  a body of knowledge that teachers plan for their students to acquire.  You should definitely not provide a lesson or a project as your curricular aim and you should probably not use an affective outcome as your curricular aim. You should define what Popham describes as a “broad scope” curricular aim.  (see Curricular Aim FAQs)

4.  Define your INSTRUCTIONAL CONTEXT. Provide some context for your curricular aim by describing a lesson or project that you might assign to accomplish your curricular aim.

Concepts

5.  Locate a RELEVANT EDUCATIONAL STANDARD. Locate a relevant set of educational standards and identify the standard that is most relevant to your Curricular Aim. If you are a K-12 teacher this should be pretty straightforward. If not you will have to be creative.  If you are a US K-12 Teacher you should almost certainly use the new Common Core or the Next Generation Science standards.  If your state is transitioning, you should consider both existing state standard AND a common core standard. But there are standards out there for almost everything and you should be familiar with them if there are.  You may have to use Google and dig around.

After you locate a standard, copy and paste the actual standard into your Wikifolio page. Then, add a link to the standard and say a few things about it. Try to explain the scope and grain size (e.g., “one of ten standards that make up....”).  Be sure to mention whether the standard is a content standard or a performance standard (see Popham, Chapter 2. This webpage will also provide a simple distinction between the two).  If you are a US math or language arts teacher, read about the common core standards and say a few things about your standard.

6.  Apply WHAT-TO-ASSESS CONSIDERATIONS.  Chapter Two details things that you should consider when deciding what to assess.  Review the following concepts and then list them in order of most relevant to least relevant, given your role and setting.

  • Curricular Aims developed by you, for your class
  • Content Standards mandated by the state
  • Content Standards suggested by national subject-matter organizations
  • NAEP assessment frameworks
  • Advice from colleagues

Add brief justifications for the What-to-Assess Consideration that you chose as most relevant and the one you chose as least relevant, keeping in mind your role and context.

Big Ideas

7.  Describe the three most relevant BIG IDEAS about deciding what to assess. Number the ideas (1, 2, 3) and list them in order of importance to you, keeping in mind your role and context. Write a brief summary/explanation of each idea where you make it clear why they are relevant to you. These are the BIG ideas of the chapter not the more specific concepts listed above. You should refer to the What Teacher Need to Know…. section and the summary at the end of Chapter 2. Try to insert your experience and academic domain into this discussion.

Self-Check (Optional)

8.  Take the SELF-CHECK.  Answer questions 1-6 without looking up the answers. If you can't answer the items you should go back over that section. Add an an entry in your wikifolio saying which ones you got wrong and why..

Pondertime (Optional)

9. Ponder the PONDERTIME questions.  Do so in light of your role and and aspirations and identify the question that is most relevant to you.  Add an entry for the most relevant question and state the question and explain why it is most relevant.

Interaction (Required)

10. Question, Discuss, ENDORSE, AND PROMOTE. (After the first deadline). After drafts are up, go in and look at your classmates wikifolios and leave comment or suggestions at the bottom.  Each week after completing your draft you should:

A. Question.  Post at least one question to your classmates that this assignment raised for you to help get discussion started.

B. Discuss.  Engage in threaded discussions (see Discussion FAQ)

C. ENDORSE.  While you are commenting, you should endorse at least three of your classmates wikifolios for being complete.  Simply post a comment starting with one ampersand (&) for completing only the required parts and and two ampersands (&&) for completing optional parts as well. (Reflections don’t count; see the Endorsement FAQ)

D. PROMOTE.  After reviewing and discussing, promote one (and only one) wikifolio for being an exemplary effort from your perspective.  Post a comment starting with three ampersands (&&&) and indicate what was exemplary about it.  (see the Promotion FAQ)

Insert an entry in your wikifolio indicating whose wikifolios you endorsed as being complete and whose wikifolio you promoted as being exemplary.

Reflection (Optional)

11. Reflect on your engagement (within one week of the original deadline).  In order to receive credit for this wikifolio, you must post three brief reflections at the bottom of the wikifolio.  You must have a coherent reflection that demonstrate understanding of the concepts and how they related to your curricular aims and context.

A. CONSEQUENTIAL ENGAGEMENT. What might you do differently after reading about this week’s topic and completing this assignment? (see Consequences FAQ)

B. CRITICAL ENGAGEMENT. How well-suited was your role and curricular aim to learning this week’s concepts and big ideas?  Did one of your classmates have a better context for learning some of these concepts?  Note that you are NOT being asked to critique how well you did.  Rather you are reflecting on the fit between your aim and role and the concepts we learned this week. (see Critiques FAQ)

C. COLLABORATIVE ENGAGEMENT. Whose comments and examples were particularly helpful for your completing this assignment?  Be specific and name people and what they contributed that was helpful.

You are advised to begin drafting the reflections while you are working on the wikifolio, and then to complete them before starting the next assignment.  They should serve as a review and help you better connect the ideas of the chapter to your practice.

Curricular Aim FAQs

You should explain a Curricular Aim that will become the basis of discussion for future Wikifolios.  This is an assessable (able to be measured by an assessment) learning outcome that covers perhaps five hours of instruction.  Your aim should reflect your role in education and ideally it will be something that your groupmate’s will find interesting and worthy of comment, and something that lends itself to a variety of assessments.
