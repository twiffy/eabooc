Overview

In this assignment, you will complete your first “Wikifolio.”  You will further refine the Curricular Aim that you drafted when you registered and further define the Context in which you use educational assessment.  You will then identify an educational standard that is aligned to your Curricular Aim and apply some of the concepts in Chapter Two. After that you will discuss and endorse Wikifolios by posting comments. Finally, you will reflect your engagement in this assignment.  Here are some pointers:

  • To fully succeed with this assignment, read Chapter 2 in Popham’s text Classroom Assessment: What Teachers Need to Know.
  • You will find the instructions for each part of the assignment by clicking on the header or arrow.
  • If you get confused look at the Teaching Assistants’ example Wikifolios or the Wikifolios of your classmates.

Curricular Aim

Restate and refine the Curricular Aim you drafted when you registered. Popham describes the “curricular aim” as the desired outcomes of instruction. This is not the “educational objectives,” “instructional objectives,” and “behavioral objectives,” but rather the measurable learning goals. In very simple terms, a Curricular Aim can be viewed as a skill or  a body of knowledge that teachers plan for their students to acquire.  You should definitely not provide a lesson or a project as your curricular aim and you should probably not use an affective outcome as your curricular aim. You should define what Popham describes as a “broad scope” curricular aim.  

Your Curricular Aim will become the basis of discussion for future Wikifolios.  This is an assessable (able to be measured by an assessment) learning outcome that covers perhaps five hours of instruction.  Your aim should reflect your role in education and ideally it will be something that your groupmate’s will find interesting and worthy of comment, and something that lends itself to a variety of assessments.

CONSIDER THE CONCEPTS (ALL REQUIRED BY MAY 18)

Relevant Educational Standard

Locate a relevant set of educational standards and identify the standard that is most relevant to your Curricular Aim. If you are a K-12 teacher this should be pretty straightforward. If not you will have to be creative.  If you are a US K-12 Teacher you should almost certainly use the new Common Core or the Next Generation Science standards.  If your state is transitioning, you should consider both existing state standard AND a common core standard. But there are standards out there for almost everything and you should be familiar with them if there are.  You may have to use Google and dig around. Start by looking at the links that your classmates added to the Educational Standards Resource Page.

After you locate a standard, copy and paste the actual standard into your Wikifolio page. Then, add a link to the standard and say a few things about it. Try to explain the scope and grain size (e.g., “one of ten standards that make up....”).  Be sure to mention whether the standard is a content standard or a performance standard (see Popham, Chapter 2. This webpage will also provide a simple distinction between the two).  If you are a US math or language arts teacher, read about the common core standards in the text and say a few things about your standard.

Reflection (by May 25)

Reflect on your engagement (within one week of the original deadline).  Post three brief reflections. You should write a coherent reflection that demonstrates understanding of the concepts and how they related to your curricular aims and context. On your Wikifolio, be sure to label each of the three types of engagement as you present your reflection.

A. Consequential Engagement. What might you do differently after reading about this week’s topic and completing this assignment?

B. Critical Engagement. How well-suited was your role and curricular aim to learning this week’s concepts and big ideas?  Did one of your classmates have a better context for learning some of these concepts?  Note that you are NOT being asked to critique how well you did.  Rather you are reflecting on the fit between your aim and role and the concepts we learned this week.

C. Collaborative Engagement. Whose comments and examples were particularly helpful for your completing this assignment?  Be specific and name people and what they contributed that was helpful.

You are advised to begin drafting the reflections while you are working on the Wikifolio, and then to complete them before starting the next assignment.  They should serve as a review and help you better connect the ideas of the chapter to your practice.
